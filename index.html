What I'm Looking For

A specific kind of business.

I am not a generalist buyer. I look for essential service businesses that do physical work for other businesses and locked-in consumers — the kind of work that has to get done whether the economy is good or bad.

My ideal acquisition is a company that has been running well for years, has a loyal customer base, and is led by an owner who is ready to transition. I am not looking to flip the business. I am looking to own and operate it myself.

If your business delivers a recurring service that your customers depend on, I would like to hear from you.

My name is Adam Wenneker, and Trinity River Management is my vehicle for acquiring and operating one great business. This is not a fund. There are no outside investors to answer to and no pressure to flip what I buy.

My career started in strategy consulting at Accenture and Booz & Company, moved through operations roles at Google and Kimberly-Clark, and eventually led to running Wehner Multifamily as CEO — a large property management company with significant annual revenue. Those years taught me how businesses actually work: how to read financials honestly, how to manage people through uncertainty, and how to tell the difference between a business with real staying power and one that only looks good on paper.

A few years ago I stepped away from large organizations deliberately. I founded Arlast Capital, a private equity firm focused on real estate, because I wanted to build something of my own rather than execute someone else's strategy. That experience — starting from scratch, making decisions with my own capital at stake, and building without a corporate infrastructure behind me — changed how I think about work. Acquiring and operating a business is the natural next expression of that same drive. It is entrepreneurship by a different path.

For a seller, that background matters. You are not handing your business to someone who has only ever managed from a distance. You are handing it to me — someone who has run organizations, made payroll, managed employees, and dealt with the real problems that come with operating a business day to day.

I hold an MBA from Columbia Business School, where I graduated first in my class and was named a McGowan Fellow, and a BBA from Texas Christian University, where I also graduated first in my class. I am connected on LinkedIn and welcome a conversation with any seller or broker who wants to get to know me before talking numbers.
